# MADNET

> *A community-driven hospitality platform where reviews can't be bought and vibes can't be faked.*

---

## The Story That Started It

₹800. A café. 4.6 stars on Google.

The chai tasted like warm disappointment. The ambiance was nothing like the photos. The reviews — paid.

That's not a rare experience. That's Tuesday for millions of people across every city in India. The problem isn't bad cafés. The problem is a broken system that lets money decide what gets seen — and lets everyone else pay the price.

---

## The Problem

Every major hospitality platform shares the same structural flaw: **the loudest voice is the one that paid the most.**

Star ratings collapse complex human experiences into a single number. Paid promotions bury authentic venues. Fake reviews dilute trust at scale. And the user — the person making a real decision with real money — has no reliable signal to go on.

A 4.3-star café could score high on food while every review quietly mentions the noise. A hidden gem with no marketing budget gets ranked below a mediocre competitor that paid for visibility. A place that's perfect for a late-night conversation gets recommended to someone looking for a Sunday brunch spot.

The data exists. The community exists. The trust infrastructure doesn't.

**That's the gap MADNET is built to close.**

---

## What MADNET Is

MADNET is a community-driven hospitality discovery platform built on one principle: **reviews should be earned, not bought.**

No paid rankings. No promoted results. No star inflation. Just real people tagging real places and rating how it made them feel.

The core innovation: replacing the broken 5-star system with a **Vibe Meter** — five emotional states that capture the actual human experience of a place, aggregated live on every venue page.

| Vibe | Signal |
|---|---|
| 😞 **Poor** | Not worth it |
| 😐 **Meh** | Below expectations |
| 🙂 **Okay** | Decent, nothing special |
| 😊 **Good** | Worth coming back |
| 🤩 **Superb** | Tell everyone |

---

## MVP — Three Features. Nothing More.

> *A product that does three things perfectly beats one that does ten things poorly.*

**01 — Vibe Meter Review System**
Users select one emotional state when posting. The aggregate Vibe Score updates live on every venue page. No stars. No paid tiers. No way to buy a better score.

**02 — Venue Discovery Feed**
A location-aware feed of nearby venues, filterable by vibe type, budget range, and place category. Every venue card leads with its live Vibe Score — not its ad spend.

**03 — User Posts with Mandatory Venue Tagging**
Every post requires a venue tag before publishing. The post appears on the user's profile and the venue's page simultaneously. Every post is automatically a verified data point.

## Who It's For

**The Explorer** — navigating a new city or neighbourhood without a trusted recommendation.
`Job to be done:` *Find a place that matches my mood, not someone's marketing budget.*

**The Social Sharer** — already posting about food and experiences, wants content that outlasts a 24-hour story.
`Job to be done:` *Have my honest post reach people who are actually looking for it.*

**The Venue Owner** — running an independent café or restaurant that delivers real quality but can't compete with paid promotion.
`Job to be done:` *Have a great experience reflected honestly in my online presence.*

## North Star Metric

**Weekly Active Reviewers** — users who publish at least one tagged venue post per week.

| Supporting Metric | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Venue pages with 5+ authentic reviews | Measures platform depth |
| Post completion rate | Measures friction in the review flow |
| Day-7 retention | Measures habit formation |
| Search-to-discovery conversion | Measures core product value |
